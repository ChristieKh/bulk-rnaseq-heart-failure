## What are iPSC-derived neurons?

Induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs) are cells that have been reprogrammed into a stem-like state. These cells can then be differentiated into specific cell types, including neurons.

This makes iPSC-derived neurons a useful human model for studying neurological disease mechanisms in a controlled laboratory setting.

## Why use them in Alzheimer’s research?

Alzheimer’s disease affects neuronal function long before large-scale tissue damage becomes visible. Studying neurons that carry Alzheimer’s-associated mutations helps researchers investigate early molecular changes linked to disease biology.

In this dataset, human iPSC-derived neurons carry mutations in genes associated with Alzheimer’s disease, including APP and PSEN1. RNA-seq allows us to examine how these mutations influence gene-expression patterns.

## Why this model is useful

Compared with postmortem brain tissue, iPSC-derived neurons offer a simpler and more controlled system. They do not capture the full complexity of the human brain, but they are useful for studying cell-intrinsic transcriptomic changes associated with disease-relevant mutations.

## Project focus

This project focuses on one simple contrast: wild-type neurons versus APP mutant neurons.

The goal is not to reproduce the full biological story of the original paper, but to build a clean and interpretable transcriptomic analysis that connects gene-expression changes to neuronal biology.
